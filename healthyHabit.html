The Importance of Rest

Muscle Recovery

  • Muscles repair during rest, not workouts
  • 24-48 hours needed between training same muscle groups
  • Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep

Sleep Essentials

  • 7-9 hours nightly for optimal recovery
  • REM sleep crucial for CNS recovery
  • Keep bedroom cool (18-20°C/65-68°F)

Active Recovery

  • Light cardio increases blood flow
  • Yoga/mobility work on rest days
  • Deload weeks every 6-8 weeks

Signs You Need More Rest

  • Persistent muscle soreness
  • Decreased performance
  • Irritability/poor sleep
  • Elevated resting heart rate

Rest days are when muscles rebuild stronger - don't skip them

The Effect of Electrolytes

Key Electrolytes for Lifters

Sodium

  • Maintains fluid balance
  • 500-700mg lost per hour sweating
  • Enhances carb absorption

Potassium

  • Prevents muscle cramps
  • Works with sodium for hydration
  • Bananas, potatoes, avocados

Magnesium

  • 300-400mg daily recommended
  • Reduces muscle spasms
  • Improves sleep quality

For workouts >90 minutes, consider electrolyte replacement drinks

Balancing your electrolytes is important in daily life.

Sunlight Exposure

Vitamin D Benefits

  • Enhances calcium absorption
  • Supports immune function
  • Linked to testosterone production

Optimal Exposure

  • 10-30 minutes midday sun
  • Arms/legs exposed if possible
  • SPF after initial exposure

Winter Alternatives

  • Vitamin D3 supplements
  • Light therapy lamps
  • Fatty fish consumption

Sunlight & Performance

  • Morning light regulates circadian rhythm
  • UV exposure increases nitric oxide (vasodilation)
  • Outdoor training provides dual benefits

Combine sunlight exposure with outdoor workouts when possible
