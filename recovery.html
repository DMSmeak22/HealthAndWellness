How The Muscles Recover

Muscle Repair Process

  • Microtears from lifting trigger repair
  • 24-48 hour recovery window post-workout
  • Protein synthesis peaks 24 hours after training

Recovery Factors

  • Protein intake (1.6-2.2g/kg body weight)
  • 7-9 hours quality sleep nightly
  • Active recovery (light movement)

Supplemental Support

  • Creatine: aids ATP regeneration
  • Omega-3s: reduce inflammation
  • BCAAs: may reduce soreness

Recovery Timeline

0-30 minutes post-workout: Glycogen replenishment starts

2-4 hours: Protein synthesis increases

24-48 hours: Full muscle repair occurs

Use these tips to make your muscle recovery quicker and more effective.

Warmup Stretches

Dynamic Warmup Routine

5-10 Minutes Light Cardio

  • Rowing machine
  • Jump rope
  • Brisk walking

Dynamic Stretches (10 reps each)

  • Arm circles
  • Leg swings
  • Bodyweight squats
  • Lunges with twist

Movement Prep

  • 2 sets of light weight for first exercise
  • Gradually increase to working weight

Never skip warmups - they increase blood flow and reduce injury risk by 30%

Some basic stretches for everyday, not just in the gym

Cooling Down

Post-Workout Routine

  • 5-10 minutes light cardio
  • Static stretching (hold 30 sec)
  • Foam rolling target areas

Recovery Techniques

  • Cold therapy (ice baths)
  • Compression garments
  • Massage therapy

Hydration & Nutrition

  • Drink 500ml water post-workout
  • 3:1 carb:protein ratio meal
  • Electrolyte replacement

Static Stretch Examples

  • Hamstring stretch (30 sec each leg)
  • Chest doorway stretch
  • Quadriceps hold
  • Child's pose for back

Proper cooldown reduces DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)
